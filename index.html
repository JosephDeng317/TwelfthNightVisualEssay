INTRODUCTION

Twelfth Night is a Shakespearean play that falls under the categories of romance and comedy. Although it is more of a lighthearted piece, there still are some serious motifs that can be picked out from it, with one of them being fake identity. There are multiple examples of people hiding under fake identities which show that many people do certain things, not for themselves, but to seek validation or acceptance from others.

SUBTOPIC 1

LIVING A LIE TO BE ACCEPTED BY SOCIETY

(hover me)

Sometimes, people are forced to hide their true identity simply because it’s unsafe to be themselves in the environment they are in.

play example

Malvolio changing the way he acts to try and impress Olivia

Viola:

(1, 2, 56-63)

Viola wants to serve Orsino but she can’t since she’s a woman. To overcome this, she has to hide under a male identity so that she could be accepted in Orsino’s court. This shows that sometimes society places restrictions on certain groups of people that may not be completely fair, which then forces people to create fake identities in order to accomplish what they want.

CONCLUSION

Despite its age, many Shakespearean plays still hold valuable lessons that can be applied in the 21st century, Twelfth Night is no exception. Much can be learned about how people build fictional identities and why they want to do so in the first place. By looking at the issues that fakeness caused in the play and relating back to the real world, progress can be made to make the world more authentic, where people can feel comfortable with who they really are.
